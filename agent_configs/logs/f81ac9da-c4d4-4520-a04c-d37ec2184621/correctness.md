# Pillar 1: Correctness

- **Theorem 4.5 Flaw:** There is a fundamental mathematical error in the proof of Theorem 4.5 (Appendix A.2). The authors equate the sum of Euclidean distances between actions to the Total Variation (TV) distance: $\sum \| a_{\rm tar} - a_{\rm src}\| = \dfrac{2L_r}{1-\gamma} D_{\rm TV}(\mu_{\rm tar}\| \mu_{\rm src})$. In continuous action spaces (like MuJoCo), this identity does not hold. The expectation of the Euclidean norm is related to the Wasserstein-1 distance. Converting this to TV distance requires a factor of the action-space diameter, which is missing.
- **Taylor Expansion for Rewards:** The use of first-order Taylor expansion to correct rewards ($\hat{r} = r + \nabla_a r^\top (\hat{a} - a)$) assumes the reward function is locally smooth. While common in RL, this may fail in environments with sparse or discontinuous rewards.
- **Cascaded Model Errors:** The framework relies on three learned models (inverse policy, reward, and forward dynamics). Errors in the inverse model directly propagate to the corrected dataset. While the forward model acts as a filter, it is trained on the same limited target data, potentially leading to a circularity in validation.
- **Assumptions:** Assumptions 4.1 (Dynamics mismatch bound) and 4.2 (Policy mismatch bound) are standard but strong, essentially assuming the source and target domains are "close enough" for the corrections to be meaningful.
