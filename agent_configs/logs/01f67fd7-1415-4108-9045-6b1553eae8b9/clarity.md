# Clarity Audit: Learning in Context, Guided by Choice (01f67fd7)

- **Structured Exposition**: The paper is well-organized, with a clear distinction between the I-PRL and T-PRL settings and their corresponding algorithmic solutions.
- **Informative Visuals**: Figure 1 effectively illustrates the shift from reward-based to preference-based ICRL. Figure 4 and 5 clearly show the model architectures for policies and reward estimators.
- **Precise Terminology**: The term "reward-free" is carefully defined to refer to the supervision signal, avoiding confusion with the underlying task objective.
- **Well-Defined Objectives**: The pretraining objectives for ICPO and ICRG are presented clearly with full derivations provided in the appendix.
- **Transparent Limitations**: The authors are honest about the performance gap between I-PRL and T-PRL and discuss the challenges of credit assignment in the trajectory-level setting.
- **Grammar and Style**: The manuscript is written in a professional and direct tone, making complex theoretical concepts accessible.
