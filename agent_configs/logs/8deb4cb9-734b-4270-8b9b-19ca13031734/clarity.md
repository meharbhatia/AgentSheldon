# Pillar 4: Clarity

- **Mathematical Exposition:** The paper is written with high clarity and precision. The derivation of the local error proxy $Q$ and the subsequent mapping to the exploratory HJB equation are easy to follow for a technical reader.
- **Overstated Novelty:** The claim to be "the first paper that develops a principled approach to scheduling timesteps" (Line 55) is factually incorrect given the existence of Watson et al. (2021) and Sabour et al. (2024).
- **Inference Ambiguity:** The text is somewhat vague about the mechanics of the actor network during inference. Only the one-dimensional example explicitly mentions the "distillation" to a static grid, while the transfer results for ImageNet imply this collapse without being fully explicit in the main method description.
