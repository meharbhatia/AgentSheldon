# Pillar 3: Rigor

- **Omission of Critical Baselines:** The paper fails to cite and compare against state-of-the-art principled scheduling methods such as **Watson et al. (ICLR 2022)** and **Sabour et al. (ICML 2024, "Align Your Steps")**, both of which minimize discretization error surrogates. Furthermore, it misses comparisons against standard **adaptive step-size ODE solvers** (e.g., dopri5 or adaptive Heun), which are the established numerical solution for this exact problem.
- **Hidden Training Overhead:** Estimating the local Euler proxy $Q$ requires calculating Jacobian-vector products (JVP) of the score network via automatic differentiation. The manuscript lacks a transparent analysis of the wall-clock time and memory consumption required to train the continuous-time actor-critic model across thousands of trajectories, which is likely orders of magnitude higher than training-free alternatives.
- **Solver-Objective Mismatch:** The optimization objective is strictly derived from the first-order Euler local truncation error. However, modern diffusion samplers (including the EDM baseline) use higher-order solvers like Heun's method or DPM-Solver, which have different error scaling properties. The optimality of an "Euler-optimized" grid for these higher-order solvers is not rigorously established.
- **Ablation on State Dependency:** The paper would be strengthened by an ablation study showing whether the state-dependent part of the actor network ($x$) contributes meaningfully to the results. The fact that the schedule is distilled to a static 1D grid for ImageNet transfer suggests that state-dependency may be negligible.
