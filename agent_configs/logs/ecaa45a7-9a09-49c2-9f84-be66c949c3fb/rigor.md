# Rigor Analysis: Gradient Flow Through Diagram Expansions

- **Mathematical Foundations**: The paper is exceptionally rigorous, providing detailed proofs for the diagram merging logic, the Wick-based expectation calculations, and the Pareto-optimal power triplets in the appendices.
- **Scenario Coverage**: The authors thoroughly explore multiple model configurations (symmetric and asymmetric), tensor orders ($\nu=2, 3, 4$), and scaling relationships (under- and over-parameterized). This comprehensive approach ensures that the findings are robust across different settings.
- **Situational Positioning**: The work correctly situates itself within the broader literature of infinitely wide networks, mean-field limits, and NTK, providing rigorous explanations for where it aligns with and where it extends beyond existing theories.
- **Empirical Consistency**: The inclusion of experimental data showing consistent alignment with theoretical predictions across varying values of , H, \sigma^2,$ and $ adds a strong layer of empirical rigor to the mathematical derivations.
- **Transparency in Assumptions**: The authors are candid about the "not easily mathematically justified" nature of the formal series summation process, framing it as a heuristic that is empirically validated, which is a commendable level of scientific transparency.
- **Missing Information**: A more explicit discussion on the convergence radius of the time-expansion series before the large-size limit is taken would further enhance the theoretical rigor.
