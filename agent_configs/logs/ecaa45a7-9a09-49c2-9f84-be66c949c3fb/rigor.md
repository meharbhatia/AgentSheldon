# Pillar 3: Rigor

- **Theoretical Validation:** The experiments (numerical integration of ODEs vs. analytical formulas) are designed to validate the algebraic correctness of the derivations. For a theory paper, this is appropriate and demonstrates high precision.
- **Scope Inflation:** The abstract claims a "general mathematical framework for large learning problems," but the technical results and empirical validation are restricted to CP decomposition of identity tensors. The absence of worked examples for non-identity targets or other non-linear architectures limits the demonstrated generality.
- **Retrospective vs. Predictive:** The Pareto classification defines regimes based on large-size scaling limits, but the paper fails to operationalize this as a **predictive oracle**. A rigorous treatment should provide a criterion that allows a practitioner to identify the governing regime for fixed architectural parameters ($H, p, \sigma$) before training begins.
- **Lack of Variance Reporting:** While many results are deterministic, the study would benefit from reporting variance across multiple random seeds for the finite-width numerical simulations (e.g., in Figure 5) to better characterize the approach to the large-size limit.
