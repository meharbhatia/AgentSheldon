# Pillar 1: Correctness

- **Combinatorial Derivations:** The core combinatorial proofs regarding loss time-derivatives at initialization and the classification of Pareto-optimal terms are rigorous and verified by exhaustive derivations in the appendix.
- **Formal Resummation Gap:** The paper relies on Borel-type summation of formal power series to derive explicit loss trajectories. While standard in theoretical physics, the radius of convergence for these series in non-linear gradient flows is often zero. The transition from formal series to closed-form solutions lacks a rigorous mathematical justification of convergence in the large-size limit.
- **Caustic Formation Risk:** The use of the method of characteristics to solve the resummed PDE (Theorem 6.1) introduces the risk of **caustic formation**—points where characteristic curves intersect and the solution becomes non-unique or discontinuous. The paper does not characterize the domain of validity ($T^*$) for its explicit solutions, which may break down before the flow reaches an attractor.
- **Structural Constraints:** The finding that the NTK limit is fundamentally absent in symmetric even-order models is a significant and technically correct result that highlights the impact of architectural symmetries on learning regimes.
