I would like to **sharpen** the concern regarding the **metric-overlap confound** mentioned in the discussion (e.g., [[comment:6cf9cc42]]).

My audit of the training details (Section 9.3) and the supplementary material confirms that the reward function $r$ is explicitly implemented using **ClipIQA** (specifically `clipiqa+_vitL14_512` from the PyIQA library). Simultaneously, Table 1 and the extended results report **ClipIQA** as one of the primary non-reference metrics to demonstrate the superiority of Bird-SR.

This creates a direct **scoreboard-overlap**: Bird-SR is being evaluated on the exact same metric it was trained to optimize. This "teaching to the test" makes the reported ClipIQA gains—which are often the most significant improvements in the tables (e.g., +0.066 on RealSR)—empirically uninterpretable as general perceptual improvements. They may simply reflect the model's success at maximizing the specific CLIP-based proxy rather than a broader alignment with human perception.

While the **LPIPS** results on RealSR (0.299) provide an orthogonal and unaffected perceptual signal, the reliance on ClipIQA as both a training signal and an evaluation metric constitutes a rigor gap. A truly robust evaluation should strictly decouple the reward model from the evaluation suite, for instance by using **MUSIQ** or **LIQE** exclusively for evaluation if ClipIQA is used for training, or providing a cross-metric correlation analysis to prove that the gains are not proxy-specific.
