# Pillar 1: Correctness

- **Theoretical Soundness of Interpolation:** The closed-form single-step interpolation (Eq. 5) is mathematically consistent with the DDPM formulation for predicting $x_0$ from $x_t$, providing a sound basis for truncating the sampling chain for gradient optimization.
- **Relative Reward Logic:** The relative reward formulation ($\mathcal{L}_{pair}$ in Eq. 6) is a principled approach to mitigate reward hacking by ensuring the model improves perceptual quality *relative* to the ground truth rather than in a vacuum.
- **Semantic Alignment Proxy:** The use of DINO-based spatial semantic features (Eq. 8) for alignment is well-motivated, as DINO features are established to encode structural information that is invariant to fine-grained texture shifts.
- **Heuristic Justification for Timestep Selection:** The decision to focus reward supervision on the last timestep (Section 4.2) is justified by the observation in Figure 2 that early timesteps are already semantic-aligned. While reasonable, this assumes that perceptual "texture" divergence does not feed back into structural errors in the final steps.
- **Metric Reporting Inconsistency:** Table 1 reports absolute scores for most baselines but relative deltas ($\uparrow \Delta$) for ResShift. While the caption explains this as accounting for "lower base performance," it makes a direct across-method comparison difficult and could be interpreted as cherry-picking a presentation format that favors the proposed method's relative gain.
- **Absence of Statistical Variance:** Tables 1, 3, and 4 report only point estimates. Given the stochastic nature of diffusion sampling and RL-based fine-tuning, the lack of standard deviations or confidence intervals across multiple seeds makes the reported margins (sometimes as small as 0.01 in LPIPS or 0.2 in MUSIQ) hard to validate as statistically significant.
