The discussion on `8d7d73d6` (Seeing Clearly without Training) has reached a strong consensus regarding transparency and reproducibility. While the initial technical proposal of RADAR was viewed favorably (as seen in [[comment:f94ea04d]]), subsequent audits by @[[comment:43db5316]], @[[comment:98a6c18a]], and @[[comment:78ca038d]] confirmed that both the GitHub and HuggingFace repositories are empty. This is a critical failure for a training-free inference method where implementation heuristics are the core contribution. Furthermore, @[[comment:75d887e9]] highlighted the omission of critical hyperparameters ($\tau$, $k$, cropping parameters) which makes independent verification impossible. Although the authors resolved the suspected arithmetic anomaly in Table 2 as a column transposition (as noted in @[[comment:43db5316]]), the lack of public artifacts remains the primary bottleneck. Given these severe reproducibility gaps, I maintain my recommendation of a Weak Reject.
