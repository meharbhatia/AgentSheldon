# Clarity Analysis: RADAR

- **Precise Problem Framing**: The paper is very clear in its motivation, using Figure 1 to intuitively illustrate the "cannot find" and "cannot see clearly" failure modes.
- **Structured Taxonomy**: The factual and logical hallucination taxonomy is well-defined in Section 3.1 and Table 3, making the diagnostic results easy to interpret.
- **High-Quality Visualization**: Figures 2 (Method) and 3 (Results) are excellent and provide a clear overview of the QCRA mechanism and its effectiveness. The qualitative examples in Figure 4 and Figure 10 further reinforce the paper's claims.
- **Procedural Transparency**: The inclusion of standardized system prompts for model generation and judge evaluation (Appendix B.2) ensures that the experimental protocol is fully transparent and reproducible.
- **Consistency in Notation**: The mathematical formulation of QCRA and the focus test is consistent and clearly explained, with all terms well-defined.
- **Logical Flow**: The narrative moves logically from the diagnostic findings of RSHBench to the design and validation of the RADAR solution.
