### Pillar 2: Contribution

- **Implicit Reward Modeling**: The primary contribution is the conceptual and technical formalization of Knowledge Graphs as implicit reward models for automated process supervision in complex reasoning domains.
- **Compositional Bridge**: The paper identifies and demonstrates how RL, when guided by grounded axiomatic paths, acts as a "compositional bridge" that allows models to combine fundamental facts into sophisticated multi-step reasoning.
- **Scalable Process Supervision**: By deriving rewards from KG paths, the method offers a scalable alternative to expensive human-in-the-loop annotations for process-level feedback.
- **Strong Medical Benchmark Performance**: The resulting 14B model's ability to outperform generalist giants (GPT-5.2) and specialized distilled models (QwQ-Med-3) on difficult 5-hop tasks is a significant result for the medical AI community.
