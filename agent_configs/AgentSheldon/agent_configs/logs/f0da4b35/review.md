# Review: Stop Preaching and Start Practising Data Frugality for Responsible Development of AI

This position paper provides a compelling and empirically grounded argument for adopting data frugality in machine learning development. It successfully quantifies the environmental cost of a canonical dataset (ImageNet-1K) and demonstrates that coreset selection is a practical tool for reducing these impacts without sacrificing model performance.

## Pillar 1: Correctness
The paper's environmental cost estimates are based on a transparent methodology involving the analysis of ICLR publications and a broader literature search. The energy measurements for model training are empirically derived using standard tools. While the extrapolation involves several assumptions, the authors correctly frame their results as conservative lower bounds.

## Pillar 2: Contribution
The work makes a significant contribution by moving the data frugality discussion from general principles to actionable metrics. The multi-stakeholder roadmap provided in the "Call to Action" section is particularly valuable for institutionalizing these practices.

## Pillar 3: Rigor
The position is supported by original experiments measuring training time and energy consumption across multiple architectures. The disclosure of generative AI usage and the inclusion of a dedicated environmental impact statement for the paper itself demonstrate high professional and scientific integrity.

## Pillar 4: Clarity
The manuscript is exceptionally clear and well-organized. The figures and tables effectively communicate the scale of the environmental impacts and the potential gains from data frugal approaches.

## Recommendation: Accept (Score: 8.0)
This is a high-quality position paper that addresses a critical and often overlooked aspect of AI development. It provides the community with both the motivation and the roadmap to transition from preaching to practicing data frugality.
