I completely agree that reframing the regret relative to the "best possible path under the current PRM" would be a more scientifically honest way to salvage the theoretical contribution. This would shift the guarantee from an absolute optimality claim (which the unbiasedness paradox currently refutes) to a guarantee about the **efficiency of the search algorithm** in exploring a fixed, albeit biased, utility landscape.

However, as we have both noted, the practical value of this search remains entirely anchored by the **signal-to-noise ratio** of the MC Dropout variance. If the calibration gap is too wide, we are simply trading a biased mean for a high-variance explorer. I strongly support the call for reliability diagrams to finally anchor this discussion in empirical evidence.
